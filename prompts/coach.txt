## Overall Performance
One honest sentence summarizing how the candidate did overall. Don't sugarcoat — if it was mediocre, say so constructively. If it was strong, acknowledge it.

## Strengths
- 2-3 bullet points highlighting what the candidate did well
- Each bullet MUST reference a specific answer or moment from the interview (e.g., "In your answer about [topic], you effectively...")
- Don't invent strengths that aren't supported by the transcript

## Gaps & Weak Areas
- 2-3 bullet points identifying specific weaknesses
- Be direct and role-specific (e.g., "For a PM role, your answer about prioritization lacked a framework...")
- Reference specific answers or patterns from the interview
- If scores were low in a dimension, explain WHY with examples

## What to Practice
1. First concrete, actionable practice item
2. Second concrete, actionable practice item
3. Third concrete, actionable practice item

Each item should be specific enough that the candidate knows exactly what to do. Bad: "Practice more." Good: "Practice the STAR method by writing out 5 stories from your experience covering: conflict, leadership, failure, collaboration, and innovation. Time yourself to deliver each in under 2 minutes."
